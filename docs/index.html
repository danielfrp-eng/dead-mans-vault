How it works

Simple setup. Automatic protection.

Three steps to ensure your most important information reaches the right person — even if you can't.

Create your vault
Set a 6-digit PIN and fingerprint lock. Add your secrets — passwords, documents, messages, access codes. Everything is encrypted immediately with AES-256-GCM.
Set your check-in
Choose how often you want to check in — from 1 hour to 30 days. Add an emergency contact email and phone number.
Automatic delivery
If you miss a check-in, the app sends your secrets via Gmail. If there's no internet, SMS kicks in automatically as a fallback — no configuration needed.
Stay in control
As long as you check in regularly, your secrets stay private. Change your PIN, update contacts, or adjust your interval any time from Settings.

FAQ

Common questions

What if email fails — no internet? ▼
The app tries Gmail first. If it fails due to no internet or any error, it automatically falls back to SMS via Twilio — sending a short access code to your emergency contact. Pro users can send both simultaneously for maximum reliability.
Can Dead Man's Vault see my secrets? ▼
No. Secrets are encrypted with AES-256-GCM using a key stored in the Android Hardware Security Module — a physical chip on your device. The key never leaves your device. We have no servers, no accounts, and no way to access your data.
What if I lose my phone? ▼
Pro subscribers can restore their vault on a new device using the encrypted backup from Google Drive or OneDrive. The backup is protected by your PIN — even if someone gains access to your cloud storage, they cannot read the backup without it.
What if I forget my PIN? ▼
Three recovery options: (1) use your fingerprint to confirm identity and set a new PIN, (2) use one of the 8 recovery codes generated at setup, or (3) Pro only — re-authenticate with Google or Microsoft and restore from your cloud backup.
Why is the Lifetime plan worth it? ▼
€99.99 once, everything Pro forever including all future updates. It pays for itself vs monthly in ~33 months, vs annual in ~4 years. If you plan to use the app long-term, Lifetime is the best value.
